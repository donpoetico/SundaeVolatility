# Concept Art Briefs — Sundae Volatility

These briefs are structured for use as image generation prompts (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion) or as reference descriptions for a human artist. Each includes style direction, composition, key details, and mood.

---

## Scene 1: Shop Interior — Full View (Day 40+)

**Style:** Gravity Falls background art style. Hand-painted 2D illustration with bold outlines (3-4px). Warm saturated palette. Slightly exaggerated proportions — interactive objects are 1.5-2x oversized. Think: Mystery Shack living room meets a cozy ice cream parlor.

**Composition:** Fixed, slightly elevated perspective from behind the counter, looking out toward a large window. The counter occupies the lower third, a midground wall with shelf and corkboard the middle band, and a large multi-pane window the upper half.

**Key Elements:**
- **Counter (foreground):** Worn, warm wood surface with visible grain, scratches, and years of use. A brass cash register with flip-digit display sits on the far left, a small brass bell beside it. Center of the counter is clear (serving area). A folded newspaper sits to the right.
- **Midground wall:** Cream-colored plaster walls. Left side: four ingredient containers on a wooden shelf (vanilla, chocolate, strawberry, mint chip — each a different color, with small hanging price tags on strings). Center: a visible doorway with warm amber light spilling through from the back room. Right side: a cork bulletin board in a walnut frame with colorful paper slips pinned to it (cream, blue, earthy green, manila — various sizes).
- **Window (background):** Large window with wooden mullions dividing it into four panes. Through it: a small Pacific Northwest town main street — a few buildings, an A-frame sign across the street, overcast sky with hints of golden light. Pedestrians visible as soft silhouettes. The street has autumn leaves.
- **Atmosphere:** Warm amber interior light contrasting with cool blue-gray exterior light through the window. Cozy, inviting, slightly cluttered but organized. The feeling of a shop that has been loved for decades.

**Mood reference:** The warmth of the Mystery Shack living room + the coziness of the Kiki's Delivery Service bakery + the lived-in quality of the Steven Universe Beach House.

**Lighting:** Late afternoon / midday — warm golden light, no harsh shadows, everything feels soft and inviting.

**Do NOT include:** Any text overlays, HUD elements, health bars, or menu buttons. Everything is diegetic — information comes from the objects themselves.

## Scene 2: Shop Interior — Day 1 (Bare Minimum)

**Style:** Same as Scene 1 but the space feels emptier, quieter, waiting.

**Composition:** Same perspective. The shop is recognizably the same room but stripped down.

**Key Differences from Scene 1:**
- Only one ingredient container is lit (vanilla, pale yellow). The other three slots are present but dim and desaturated, almost ghostly — suggesting "not yet relevant."
- No corkboard on the wall — just blank cream plaster.
- No newspaper on the counter.
- No doorway visible — the wall behind the shelf is solid.
- No cuckoo clock.
- The register shows "$2,000" in flip digits.
- Through the window: a gray, quiet morning. One or two pedestrians at most. No A-frame sign across the street.
- A single elderly woman (Marta) stands at the counter — small, gentle, reading glasses, olive green coat.

**Mood:** Quiet potential. A shop that was once great and is waiting to be great again. Melancholy but hopeful. The first morning of a new beginning.

## Scene 3: Back Room

**Style:** Same Gravity Falls illustration approach but warmer and dimmer. Think: a grandfather's study or workshop, lit by a desk lamp. Darker wood tones than the shop.

**Composition:** Similar slightly elevated perspective. The room is smaller and more intimate than the shop.

**Key Elements:**
- **Grandfather's Desk (center-right, dominant):** A large, dark wood desk — scarred, stained, obviously old. Coffee ring marks on the surface. On it:
  - A leather-bound journal, open, with handwritten text in warm ink (Polish-accented script). Coffee stains on the page. Small margin sketches of ice cream cones and mathematical doodles.
  - A brass mechanical calculator — gears visible, a pull lever on the side, a narrow paper tape feeding out.
  - A worn leather tool roll with brass instruments glinting inside (caliper, spring, magnifying lens).
  - A small brass dial (Rho Dial) near the calculator.
- **Barometer (back wall, left):** An ornate wall-mounted pressure gauge — brass frame, glass face, needle pointing to zones marked by color (green, amber, red). Steam wisps rising from a small valve.
- **Aging Rack (left wall):** A simple wooden shelf structure with glass jars and containers. Some look freshly stocked (bright labels), others aged beautifully (darker, richer, with patina on the containers).
- **Supplier Board (back wall, right):** A wooden board with pinned business cards, handwritten notes, and price lists from local suppliers.
- **Desk Lamp:** A green banker's lamp casting warm, focused light on the desk. Dust motes float visibly in the beam.
- **Doorway (left edge):** An open doorway leading back to the bright shop — a sliver of the shop's warm light visible through it.

**Mood:** Contemplative, warm, mysterious. Grandfather's secrets. The place where you go to think deeply about your promises. Traces of pipe tobacco and old paper and brass polish.

## Scene 4: Corkboard Close-Up (Lean-In View)

**Style:** Same illustration approach but zoomed in — the corkboard fills most of the frame. High detail on paper textures.

**Composition:** Straight-on view of the corkboard, slightly angled. The shop is visible but blurred in the background (bokeh effect — the window light, the counter edge, all soft).

**Key Elements:**
- **The cork surface:** Visible pores, old pin holes, a walnut wooden frame with brass corner brackets.
- **Deal slips pinned in a grid (3x4):**
  - Cream cardstock with red "ORDER" stamps and red pins (Mrs. Chen's forwards)
  - Light blue cards with purple "RESERVATION" stamps and blue pins (Dmitri's reservations)
  - Earthy, textured paper with green "GUARANTEE" stamps and a leaf motif, green pins (Agnes's guarantees)
  - Thick manila folded documents with a wax "K" seal and brass pins (Kazik's compound deals)
- **Attached tools:** A brass caliper clipped to one slip (tiny gears visible, mid-spin). A coiled spring alongside it, slightly tense.
- **A calculator ticket:** A narrow receipt-style paper tape pinned as a tab on the top-right of one slip, showing small horizontal bars.
- **Below the main grid:** A narrow strip with 2 punch cards pinned — stiff cream cards with three visible rotating wheels.

**Mood:** A wall of promises. Each piece of paper is a commitment, and together they form a portfolio the player manages through physical objects. It should feel both organized and slightly overwhelming — the complexity building.

## Character 1: Marta — The Constant

**Style:** Professor Layton character design. Highly stylized, ~3-4 head proportions. Distinctive silhouette. Warm watercolor-ish shading.

**Description:** An elderly woman, small and slightly hunched, with a perpetual gentle smile. She radiates quiet warmth and reliability.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Reading glasses:** Gold wire frames, perched on the tip of her nose. The signature prop.
- **Coat:** The same muted olive green wool coat every day. Well-worn, clean, comfortable.
- **Hair:** Gray, neatly pinned up in a small bun. A few wisps escaping.
- **Purse:** A small, structured handbag — always has exact change ready.
- **Posture:** Slightly forward-leaning, attentive, unhurried.
- **Expression range:** Gentle smile (default), soft concern (when shop is struggling), quiet satisfaction (when served her vanilla).
- **Color palette:** Olive green, warm tan, soft gray, gold accents (glasses).

**Silhouette test:** Small, rounded, compact — she should read as "gentle elderly regular" even as a tiny silhouette on the corkboard.

## Character 2: Mrs. Chen — The Organizer

**Style:** Professor Layton character design. Tall, upright, angular.

**Description:** A tall, organized woman who runs the Community Center. Everything about her says "prepared" and "capable."

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Clipboard:** Always in hand — day planner or scheduling clipboard. The signature prop.
- **Blazer:** Navy blue, well-fitted, professional but not corporate.
- **Reading glasses:** On a beaded chain around her neck — uses them to inspect her own slips.
- **Hair:** Black, structured updo. Not a strand out of place.
- **Posture:** Perfectly upright. Confident. She knows what she wants.
- **Expression range:** Businesslike neutral (default), satisfied nod (deal accepted), tight-lipped disapproval (counter-offer too aggressive).
- **Color palette:** Navy blue, white, cool gray, subtle gold chain.

**Silhouette test:** Tall, vertical, angular with the clipboard extending the silhouette — reads as "authority figure with a plan."

## Character 3: Dmitri — The Cautious Baker

**Style:** Professor Layton character design. Broad, solid, slightly stooped.

**Description:** A broad-shouldered Russian immigrant who runs the bakery next door. Cautious, worried, but kind underneath.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Baker's apron:** Perpetually dusted with flour — white smudges on dark fabric. The signature prop.
- **Build:** Broad-shouldered, barrel-chested, thick hands. Built for kneading.
- **Eyebrows:** Permanently raised in worry. Endearingly anxious.
- **Clothing:** Dark work shirt under the apron. Rolled-up sleeves showing flour-dusted forearms.
- **Posture:** Slightly stooped, hands often wringing or clasped together.
- **Expression range:** Worried neutral (default), cautious hope (considering a deal), visible relief (when deal works out), slight wince (when prices are scary).
- **Color palette:** Dark brown, flour white, warm tan, muted blue shirt.

**Silhouette test:** Wide, solid, with the apron creating a distinctive trapezoidal torso shape — reads as "the baker."

## Character 4: Agnes — The Anxious Farmer

**Style:** Professor Layton character design. Angular, windswept, fidgety.

**Description:** A weathered farmer who supplies strawberries. Always slightly nervous, always in a hurry, always carrying her basket.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Strawberry basket:** Wicker basket with red strawberries visible. The signature prop. She holds it protectively.
- **Sun hat:** Pushed back on her head, wisps of hair escaping underneath.
- **Clothing:** Earth-toned practical fabrics — a canvas jacket, worn jeans, muddy boots.
- **Hands:** Work-roughened, always fidgeting — adjusting the basket handle, tucking hair.
- **Posture:** Slightly off-balance, as if she might bolt. Forward-leaning.
- **Expression range:** Nervous default (tight smile), worried glance (checking prices), grateful relief (when guarantee accepted).
- **Color palette:** Earth brown, green accents, straw yellow, strawberry red pops.

**Silhouette test:** Angular, with the hat brim and basket creating an asymmetric profile — reads as "farmer who came in from the fields."

## Character 5: Uncle Kazik — The Showman

**Style:** Professor Layton character design. Expansive, theatrical, unmistakable.

**Description:** Stanislaw's brother. A wheeler-dealer with a heart of gold and a deal always ready. Everything about him is big.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Hat:** Wide-brimmed fedora at a slightly rakish angle. The signature prop.
- **Coat:** Oversized coat with many pockets — he's always pulling things out of them.
- **Pocket watch:** Gold chain visible from vest pocket. A man who knows the value of time.
- **Hands:** Expressive, always gesturing — pointing, snapping, spreading wide to describe a deal.
- **Eyebrow:** One permanently raised. Mischievous grin.
- **Posture:** Expansive. Takes up space. Leans on the counter.
- **Expression range:** Mischievous grin (default), knowing wink (presenting a compound deal), theatrical dismay (when player declines), warm approval (when player handles a complex deal well).
- **Color palette:** Dark burgundy/brown coat, gold accents (watch, buttons), cream shirt.

**Silhouette test:** The hat + expansive coat + gesturing hands create the widest, most dramatic silhouette — reads as "the big deal."

- **Reading glasses:** Hanging from a cord around his neck (doesn't need them for chess, only for reading).

- **Color palette:** Warm browns, soft grays, oatmeal knit textures.

## Character 8: Nadia — The Rival

**Style:** Professor Layton character design but cooler palette. She's the visual contrast to everything warm about the shop.

**Description:** Runs the frozen yogurt shop across the street. Only seen through the window — she never enters. Sleek, modern, competitive.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Visible through window only:** She's seen across the street, next to her A-frame sign. Distance + window glass = softer rendering.
- **Aesthetic:** Clean, modern — the anti-Sundae Volatility. Everything about her shop is geometric where yours is organic.
- **Haircut:** Sharp bob, precise.
- **Clothing:** Fitted jacket, geometric earrings.
- **A-frame sign:** Clean modern typography listing her prices — a stark contrast to the Label Maker font on your price tags.
- **Posture:** Confident, arms crossed or adjusting her sign.
- **Color palette:** Cool grays, whites, ice blue. She's the cold note in the warm scene.

**Silhouette test:** Sharp, angular, the bob creating a distinctive head shape — reads as "the modern competitor" even through window glass.

## Key Object: The Journal

**Style:** Close-up illustration. Extreme detail on textures.

**Description:** A heavy leather-bound journal lying open on the desk. This is the emotional core of the game — Grandfather Stanislaw's life's work.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Cover (when closed):** Dark brown leather, worn and cracked. A simple embossed monogram. No title.
- **Pages (when open):** Cream aged paper with visible fiber texture. Handwritten in warm brown ink.
- **Coffee ring stains:** Multiple — some overlapping text, partially obscuring content. Different sizes, different ages.
- **Margin sketches:** Small drawings of ice cream cones, weather patterns, mathematical spirals, simple map fragments.
- **Pressed flowers:** Between some pages — a dried autumn leaf, a spring blossom. Slightly translucent against the page.
- **Cipher pages:** Some pages use a mix of Polish text, mathematical notation (integrals, sigma notation), and ice cream recipe diagrams arranged in a non-obvious pattern.
- **Water damage:** Some pages have blurred sections — the player can't read everything, and that's intentional.
- **Page edges:** Visible on the right side — the thickness tells you how much of the story has been revealed.

**Mood:** This journal should feel like holding a piece of someone's life. Every stain, every pressed flower, every marginal note is a trace of a person who loved mathematics and ice cream in equal measure.

## Key Object: The Mechanical Calculator

**Style:** Close-up illustration. Steampunk-adjacent but grounded — this is a real antique, not fantasy.

**Description:** A brass and steel calculating machine on Grandfather's desk. Think: a beautiful antique Curta calculator crossed with a receipt printer.

**Key Visual Details:**
- **Body:** Brass housing with visible screws and joints. Patina and wear.
- **Gears:** Visible through small windows in the housing. Different sizes, interlocking.
- **Pull lever:** A substantial lever on the right side — satisfying to pull.
- **Paper tape:** A narrow slot where receipt-style tickets emerge.
- **Output ticket:** Shows three small weather icons (sun, clouds, storm) with horizontal bars extending left (red) or right (green). A handwritten number at the bottom.

**Mood:** A machine from another era that somehow does exactly what you need. Beautiful, mechanical, trustworthy.
